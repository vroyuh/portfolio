MAJIA

August – December 2025

Role:

UX Researcher, UX Designer

Tools:

Figma

Overview:

MAJIA was born from my personal experience as a solo traveler. During my first solo trip, I struggled to find resources and accommodations that were tailored to the unique needs of a single traveler. Researching options for lodging, budgeting, and planning often felt overwhelming. I realized these trips could be so much easier with the right resources — so I decided to create MAJIA to help others experiencing the same pain points.

Background & Problem Statement

Solo female travelers face persistent safety concerns, higher travel-related costs, and feelings of isolation, yet current travel platforms fail to provide an integrated, trustworthy all-in-one solution.

Target Market: 18–45 year old female solo traveler, or those interested in partaking in the future.

Value Proposition: Assisting women in gaining the confidence to travel solo by providing resources that would increase their sense of security.

Persona Development

Lily

AGE28
GENDERSingle
LOCATIONNYC, NY

Jane is a 28-year-old female solo traveler who thrives on discovering new cultures and experiences. She values the independence of exploring the world on her own terms but often feels overwhelmed when planning trips. Her goal is to travel confidently and comfortably, with platforms that give her access to safe, affordable accommodation options.

GOALS

  • Confidently plan and book safe, affordable accomodations tailored to her preferences.
  • Reduce stress from travel logistics and streamline trip planning.

NEEDS

  • Verified listings with reviews geared toward solo travelers.
  • Integrated platforms for flights, lodging, and activities to reduce overwhelness.

PAIN POINTS

  • Struggles to find safe and affordable accomodations that fit her preferences.
  • Overwhelmed by the sheer volume of planning (transportation, lodging, local expereinces).
  • Frustrated by the slow process of finding resources
  • Anxious about navigating unfamiliar areas without reliable support.

Discovery interviews

To understand more about the experiences of my target audience I conducted three in person interviews with female solo travelers (all anonymized, n = 3). The goal was to uncover real hardships and pain points in their lived experienced with solo travel. I developed interview questions to guide the discussion and ensure consistency across participants.

Sample of Questions Included:

How do you go about planning a solo travel trip?

Do you have any issues that arise when you solo travel?

What's the hardest part about solo travel?

How does this issue impact your drive for solo travel?

Four key factors emerged:

  • Safety Concerns: Fear of traveling alone in unfamiliar cities, uncertainty about safe hotels and neighborhoods, limited local knowledge, and the need to remain in populated areas to feel secure.
  • Financial Challenges: Higher costs compared to group travel and difficulty finding affordable flights and accommodations.
  • Emotional Experience: Waves of loneliness and a desire for community or meaningful connection while traveling solo.
  • Lack of Effective Tools: Few apps are designed specifically for solo female travelers, and existing platforms do not combine safety, affordability, and community features.

Competitive Analysis

Nomadher Global community and app for female solo travelers. It connects women, shares experiences and advice, and promotes safe travel worldwide. Doesn't necessarily promote women to travel on their own instead travel with other women in groups.
Sisters Uncharted A platform offering female-focused travel resources, curated itineraries, and real-time support for solo women travelers. Specifically restricted for women over the age of 40.
Just Go Solo A resource hub that provides women with travel ideas, tools, and services to women-only tours.
G Adventures A global tour company offering small-group trips, including options designed for solo travelers seeking community and adventure.
Solo Female Travelers A women-only travel company offering curated group trips and a supportive community for solo female travelers.

Concept Test

View Design

To evaluate whether this new website idea would appeal to female solo travelers, I created a high fidelity landing page in Figma. I still need to recruit and conduct moderated usability tests so that's to come in the near future.

My plan outlined the following research questions:

H1: Users will find the concept of resources for different aspects of solo travel appealing.

Ask participants to rate concept appeal (1–7) and explain what feels most or least valuable.

H2: Users will express curiosity around having access to these resources.

Collect post test reflections or short emotion check-ins ("How curious do you feel to use these resources for a future solo trip?").

H3: Users will express an interest in using these resources to help plan a solo trip.

Ask behavioral intention questions: "When do you think you'd use this?" and "Walk me through how this would change your routine."

Methods: Concept Test with Prototype (Moderated, Remote, 5–10 minute sessions)

Participants will be shown a clickable high fidelity Figma prototype representing the core flow:

  1. Browse verified safe and affordable accommodations.
  2. Explore safety maps showing secure cities and neighborhoods.
  3. View and share solo travel experiences in the blog section.
  4. Read travel advice and safety tips tailored for women travelers.
